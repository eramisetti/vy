OUR VISION

We seek to promote health, happiness and harmony at all levels - physical, mental, emotional, spiritual - through yoga and mindfulness.

OUR MISSION

Our mission is to spread awareness of the benefits of yoga, active lifestyle and mindfulness to achieve a healthy balanced lifestyle by conducting classes, seminars for both adults and children.

OUR VALUES

We believe everyone has the power to help themselves. We provide them with life tools to heal, evolve and grow.

What we do
We are more than a body mind complex. However an exploration into deeper aspects of life is not possible without a strong body and a stable mind. So we seek to employ various tools envisioned by the rishis to gain physical strength and mental fortitude. Our community yoga classes focus on asanas, pranayama, relaxation, and meditation.Advanced and teacher training classes include learning of kriyas or cleansing techniques, what constitutes a healthy diet, understanding of the 8 limbs of ashtanga yoga, yogic philosophy, lectures by experts in health and medicine, Sanskrit, and an exploration of ancient texts. Overall, we look at yoga as a way of life, instead of as an hourly practice on the mat.

We seek to promote health and wellness in our community by conducting regular yoga classes and educating our participants on practices that enhance our quality of life. We practice traditional yoga neither diluting nor flavoring - to preserve and pass on this treasure trove of knowledge in its original form.

Some of our highlights include
  • Free workshops focused on prevention and management of common ailments.
  • Sessions focused on pranayama, meditation to reduce excessive mental activity and provide deep relaxation.
  • Understand and practice the philosophy of yoga from texts such as the Bhagavad Gita, Patanjali Yoga Sutras.
  • Teach basic Sanskrit to self study ancient texts.
  • Camps and classes for children to develop a strong body and sharp mind. Special focus on emotional intelligence for teenagers.
  • Collaborate with health experts, practitioners of traditional medicine and alternative medicine to find the common thread of wellness that unites all of us.

    Physical- Wellbeing

    The following practices are imparted to gain an ideal posture, gait, strength, stamina, flexibility, dexterity and suppleness of the body

    • Sithilakarana Vyayama (Loosening Exercises)
    • Sookshma Vyayama (Subtle Exercises)
    • Asana (Yoga Postures)
    • Kriyas (Yogic Cleansing practices)

    Mental – Wellbeing

    Practices to calm down and to take a break from incessant thinking. Train the mind to focus when needed and defocus at other times

    • Breathing Exercises
    • Yoga Nidra
    • Guided meditation
    • Dharana
    • Dhyana

    Emotional – Wellbeing

    Emotions are thoughts that carry a lot of charge and energy. Inability to manage one’s emotions could make one’s life dysfunctional. Equanimity can be gained by practices such as

    • Understanding the emotional scale
    • Being in the observer mode
    • Mirroring back
    • Sharing experience
